About Me

I'm a student at Northern Illinois University majoring in Operations and Information Management with a minor in Computer Science. Currently serving as a Community Ambassador for ACM (Association for Computing Machinery) at NIU, where I help foster connections within the computing community.

I'm actively engaged in multiple professional experiences, including serving as a Student Consultant for Mystery Street Recording Company through NIU's competitive Experiential Learning Center (ELC) program. In this role, I collaborate with cross-functional teams to deliver strategic marketing and HR recommendations, conducting market research and developing actionable business strategies for a Chicago-based audio studio.

As an Undergraduate Student Researcher at NIU's College of Business, I focus on using large language models (LLMs) and natural language processing (NLP) techniques to explore realism, bias, and thematic alignment between AI and human-generated text. I've developed custom GPT-based simulation frameworks and built end-to-end NLP pipelines using Python to process and analyze over 30 AI and human-generated responses.

My previous experience includes internships at Shipt as a Network Planning intern, where I designed and deployed Tableau analytical systems for delivery performance monitoring, and at Innovation DuPage as an IT Data Analyst, building automated dashboards and reporting frameworks. With this diverse background spanning consulting, research, and data analytics, I'm passionate about applying data-driven solutions to solve complex business challenges.
